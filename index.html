The Beauty of \( f(x) = \sqrt{x^2} + \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n} \)

Consider the function:

\[ f(x) = |x| + \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n} \]

While at first glance this appears to combine elementary and advanced mathematical concepts, it reveals fascinating connections between different areas of mathematics.

Component Analysis

1. Absolute Value Component:

\[ \sqrt{x^2} = |x| \]

This fundamental function demonstrates the geometric interpretation of absolute value while maintaining differentiability everywhere except at the origin.

2. Infinite Series Component:

\[ \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n} \]

The harmonic series serves as a classic example of a divergent series, with partial sums growing logarithmically:

\[ \sum_{n=1}^N \frac{1}{n} \approx \ln(N) + \gamma \]

Where \(\gamma\) is the Euler-Mascheroni constant (\(\gamma \approx 0.5772\)).

Functional Interpretation

Despite the divergence of the harmonic series, we can analyze the function's behavior through regularization:

\[ f(x) = |x| + \lim_{s\to 1^+} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^s} \]

This connects to the Riemann zeta function:

\[ \zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^s} \]

Revealing deep connections between real analysis and complex function theory.
